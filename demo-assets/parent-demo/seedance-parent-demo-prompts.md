## Core Feeling
MindCraft should feel like relief after uncertainty. The emotional pressure is real: parents do not want to watch a child drift through math, lose confidence, avoid homework, or arrive at college prep too late. But the language must never shame the student or the parent. The feeling is: """You are not helpless. You can see the next step now."""

## Seedance Prompt: 30-Second Parent Demo

Create a premium cinematic product demo video for MindCraft, a math learning support platform for parents and students.

Visual style: luxury editorial education brand, tactile paper, deep forest shadows, burgundy warmth, cream surfaces, gold architectural linework, subtle golden-ratio arcs, real iPad/product screenshots, no cartoon style, no generic tech blue gradient, no cheap emojis, no stock-photo smiles. Camera movement should be slow, grounded, and emotionally intelligent.

Music and sound: warm low piano, soft felt keys, subtle heartbeat-like bass pulse, gentle pencil-on-paper texture, quiet page turn, soft iPad tap, restrained cinematic swell at the moment the plan becomes visible. No alarm sound, no shame, no panic. The feeling should be responsibility turning into clarity.

Scene 1, 0-4s:
Late evening kitchen table. Parent sits beside student notebook. A math worksheet is open. Parent does not look defeated; they look concerned and attentive. Lamp light is warm. Obsidian green background, burgundy sweater or book accent, cream paper, gold pen.
On-screen text: """When math starts feeling uncertain, parents feel it too."""

Scene 2, 4-8s:
Close shot of parent looking at an iPad with the MindCraft dashboard. The UI should feel premium and calm: forest green, cream, burgundy, gold, clear progress map. Show abstract interface details only, no tiny unreadable clutter.
On-screen text: """MindCraft shows where your child stands."""

Scene 3, 8-13s:
Transition into the student answering a question on an iPad. The screen has a clean question workbench: question on the left, hints/calculator/tool lane nearby, plenty of space for scratch work. Show the student tapping a hint, then writing one step.
On-screen text: """Practice, hints, and homework become evidence."""

Scene 4, 13-18s:
Knowledge map animates gently. Nodes connect. One path glows softly in brand green/gold. Avoid sci-fi. Make it feel like a private learning atlas.
On-screen text: """So the next step is visible."""

Scene 5, 18-23s:
Tutor session context appears: notes, mind map, homework help, and practice history converge. Parent sees a calm summary, not a confusing report.
On-screen text: """Tutoring starts with context, not guessing."""

Scene 6, 23-30s:
Return to the warm table. Parent and student sit in quiet focus. The iPad shows a clear route, not a grade or scary score. End with MindCraft wordmark.
Final text: """Know what to do next."""
Subtext: """A calmer way to support math at home."""

Avoid:
- Do not imply the child is broken.
- Do not show panic, crying, failing grades, or shame.
- Do not overuse red as danger. Burgundy should feel warm and serious, not alarmist.
- Do not show fake charts with unreadable numbers.
- Do not use cartoon icons, lightning bolts, confetti, or cheap gamification.

## Voiceover Draft

MindCraft turns practice, homework, notes, and tutoring into one clear learning picture. It shows where your child stands, what to do next, and when a tutor can help most. Less guessing. More direction. A calmer way to support math at home."""
