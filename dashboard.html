Algebra II — Quadratic Functions

In this session, we explored quadratic functions in three forms: standard, vertex, and factored. The key insight is that all three forms represent the same parabola — choosing the right form depends on what information you need most quickly.

Key concepts covered:

1
Vertex Form

y = a(x−h)² + k — instantly tells you the vertex (h, k)

2
Factoring (FOIL)

Finding roots by expressing as (x − r₁)(x − r₂) = 0

3
Completing the Square

Transforming standard → vertex form. Needs more practice.
